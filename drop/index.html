01The setup

The banger is at the kitchen.
You're at the baseline.

Their next ball is going to be hit at a hundred miles an hour. Aimed at your feet. Or your shoulder. Or the gap between you and your partner. What do you do?

02The answer

The drop. Soft arc. Lands in their kitchen.

Don't trade pace. Take it away. The drop is a soft arcing shot from the baseline that lands one to two feet past your opponent's kitchen line. They have to hit up. The rally resets. You walk to the line.

03The target

Land it here.
Nowhere else.

There's a one-foot wide stripe of court that's perfect. It's just past their kitchen line, in the middle. Land your drop there and the rally is yours.

The Goldilocks zone

One to two feet past their kitchen line, center court. Forces a low contact — they have to hit up.

Too short — in the kitchen

The ball sits up. They reach in and put it away. The worst possible drop.

Too deep — past the kitchen

You gave them another drive. They're at the line. You're still at the baseline. Disaster.

Too high — the pop-up

Even if it lands in the right spot, a high-bouncing ball gets attacked. Apex must peak before the net.

04The mechanics

Three keys.
Get all three.

Soft hands. Under the ball. Let it come. Miss any one of them and the drop pops up. Get all three and the ball lands in the green every single time.

Your fingers are barely wrapped. The paddle absorbs the ball instead of pushing it. Soft.

Hold the paddle like a baby bird.

The single biggest pop-up cause: a death grip. Tight hands transfer energy back to the ball. Soft hands absorb it, and the ball drops dead. Sarah Ansboury says 3 out of 10. Tyler Loong says: if your knuckles are white, your drop is dead.

If you can feel the ball compress on the paddle face, you've got it right.

Tip the paddle face up.

A closed face drives. An open face drops. The contact is below the ball, paddle scoops up underneath, and the ball arcs over the net. Drag the slider — you can see the arc change in real time.

Closed face for power. Open face to lift. Drop = open. Always.

Wait for the ball. Don't chase it.

Coach Dave's mantra: let it come. Most pop-ups happen because players reach forward and meet the ball above their waist. The drop wants contact at mid-thigh, paddle in front, body still. If you have to lunge, you've already lost the shot.

Take it after the bounce. Let it drop into the strike zone. Hit it from underneath.
05The decision

Drop or drive?
Two questions decide.

Every third shot you ever play is one of these two answers. The decision is mechanical. Where did the ball land, and how high is it when you take it. That's it.

Question 1
Where did their return land?
▼ DROP

Deep return.
Past the service line.

✓You're behind the baseline. You're not advancing in time.
✓The ball is below your waist when you reach it.
✓Their team is already locked at the kitchen.
▶ DRIVE

Short return.
Inside the transition zone.

✓The ball is high. You're taking it above your waist.
✓You're already moving forward. Pace adds threat.
✓Their team is still getting to the kitchen.
Question 2  ·  the override
Below your waist?  →  Drop. Always. Even if it's short.
06The reset

Caught in the middle?
Reset.

The drop's twin. Same physics, played from no-man's land. Compact paddle, square face, soft hands. The ball comes at you fast. You barely move. It dies in their kitchen.

01
Compact

Paddle barely moves. Six inches max. No backswing.

02
Square face

Paddle face flat. Slight upward tilt. Acts like a wall.

03
Absorb

Soft hands kill the pace. The ball drops dead off your face.

04
Then walk

After the reset, take two steps forward. The kitchen is your reward.

07The traps

The three mistakes
that kill every drop.

When your drop misses, it misses one of three ways. Diagnose which one. The fix is always the opposite of the mistake.

⚠ MISTAKE 01
The pop-up

Your drop arcs too high. Apex is over the net or past it. Bouncing high in the kitchen, opponent steps in and crushes it.

Fix Soften the grip. Quiet the wrist. Let the shoulder do the work. Apex must peak before the net.
⚠ MISTAKE 02
Sailing long

Closed paddle face. Too much pace. The ball flies past their kitchen line and lands deep. They drive it back at you.

Fix Open the paddle face. Take 30% off the swing. Aim for the kitchen line, not the baseline.
⚠ MISTAKE 03
The net dive

You decelerated. The paddle stopped moving at contact. The arc had no energy — it nosedives into the net.

Fix Smooth low-to-high follow-through. Brush up through the ball. Finish with the paddle pointing at your target.
